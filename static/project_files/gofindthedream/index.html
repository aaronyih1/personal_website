Gofindthedream.com was an exploration into what people's dreams were, and how we could help make them happen.

Link to inactive but interactive site.

We presented users with two textfields: 1. for them to detail their hopes and dreams and 2. for their email address. We provided no instructions except for "let's chase your dream...describe it here".

We had around 60 responses. Some of them were pretty shocking, some of them heartwarming, and others, still, just totally spammy.

We responded to their dream requests with resources and roadmaps on how to make their dreams a reality. Many of the recipients did not reply to the first message, and of the few who did, only a small fraction continued to converse with us, and all of them stopped responding sooner or later.

Dreams motivate us; they give us purpose and meaning, enabling us to become the best version of ourselves. As a result, dreams are deeply personal, private forces in our lives. For that reason, I was surprised to find how open people were to sharing not only their dreams but a method of contact, too.
